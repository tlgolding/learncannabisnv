Limonene

Fresh, slightly citrusy

About:

  • From the Italian word limone
  • Steam distilled – peels are boiled, steam is collected and separated
  • Flammable and toxic to aquatic life
  • Can be used as biofuel
  • Solvent – fragrant alternative to turpentine

Effects

  • Euphoric/mood-lifting
  • Usually reduces stress and anxiety, can induce anxiety in some

Practical Applications

  • Anti-inflammatory/pain reliever
  • Antibacterial/Antifungal/Antimicrobial
  • Anticancer
  • Pesticide/Insect repellant
  • Flavoring for food, candy, gum, etc.
  • Fragrance in perfumes/cosmetics
  • Fragrance in cleaners and industrial fluids
  • Opens BBB

Unique Applications

  • Suppresses appetite and supports healthy digestion
  • Prevents some of the negative side effects of chemo
  • Lowers blood sugar, triglycerides, cholesterol
  • Analgesic in large doses

Also Found In:

  • citrus fruit peels and juice
  • orange peel contains up to 97% limonene
  • red and silver maple trees
  • cottonwoods and apsens
  • sumac, spruce, douglas fir
  • juniper
  • larches, cedars
